# Forklift Pre-Shift Inspection

**Department:** Warehouse
**Revision:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** 2026-01-01

---

## Purpose

Ensure every forklift is safe to operate before each shift. Operators must complete this inspection and report any defects before moving the vehicle.

---

## Required Equipment

- Inspection clipboard and checklist form
- Personal protective equipment (PPE): safety vest, steel-toed boots, hard hat
- Tagging kit (red out-of-service tags)

---

## Steps

1. **Park safely before inspecting.**
   Position the forklift on level ground with the forks lowered, parking brake engaged, and engine off.

2. **Check fluid levels.**
   - Engine oil — must be between MIN and MAX marks.
   - Coolant — reservoir must be at least half full.
   - Hydraulic fluid — check sight glass; top off if below MIN.
   - Battery water (electric models) — fill to manufacturer line with distilled water only.

3. **Inspect tires.**
   Walk around the vehicle. Look for cuts, chunking, or flat spots. Pneumatic tires must not be more than 25% flat. Solid tires must not show exposed cords.

4. **Test the horn and lights.**
   Turn the key to the ON position (engine off). Press the horn — must be audible from 20 feet. Confirm all lights (headlights, reverse alarm, strobe) function.

5. **Check forks and carriage.**
   - Forks must be straight, no cracks at the heel or tips.
   - Fork spread must be even — tolerance ±0.5 in.
   - Carriage chains must be taut and equally tensioned.

6. **Inspect the mast.**
   Raise mast to full height and lower slowly. Watch for jerking, side-to-side play, or unusual noise. Check for frayed lift chains.

7. **Test brakes.**
   Start the engine. Drive forward at low speed (walking pace) in a clear aisle, then apply service brake firmly. The vehicle must stop within one full vehicle length.

8. **Verify the data plate is legible.**
   The rated capacity and load center must be readable. If the plate is missing or illegible, remove the forklift from service immediately.

9. **Complete and sign the inspection form.**
   Record date, shift, unit number, and operator name. Note any defects in the "remarks" section.

10. **Report defects before operating.**
    Any item marked FAIL must be reported to the supervisor and the forklift tagged out of service with a red tag before it is moved.

---

## Warnings

> **WARNING — Do not operate a forklift with a failed inspection item.** A tagged-out forklift may not be moved except by maintenance personnel performing repairs.

> **WARNING — Never add water to a battery that is not fully charged.** Overfilling a charged battery can cause acid overflow and chemical burns.

---

## Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Carriage | The vertical assembly that holds the forks and moves up and down the mast. |
| Mast | The vertical uprights and rails that guide the carriage during lifting. |
| Pneumatic tire | An air-filled rubber tire (as opposed to a solid cushion tire). |
| Out-of-service tag | Red tag hung on the steering wheel indicating the unit must not be operated. |
