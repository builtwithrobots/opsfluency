# Chemical Handling and Storage

**Department:** Safety
**Revision:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** 2026-01-01

---

## Purpose

Protect employees from injury and comply with OSHA Hazard Communication Standard (29 CFR 1910.1200) by establishing consistent procedures for receiving, labeling, storing, and disposing of chemicals on site.

---

## Scope

Applies to all employees who handle, move, or work near any chemical substance — including cleaning products, lubricants, adhesives, and industrial fluids.

---

## Required PPE

| Task | Minimum PPE |
|---|---|
| Receiving shipments | Safety glasses, nitrile gloves |
| Dispensing or mixing | Face shield, chemical-resistant gloves, apron |
| Cleaning up a spill | Full face shield, nitrile gloves, apron, closed-toe shoes |
| Disposing of containers | Nitrile gloves, safety glasses |

---

## Steps

### Receiving Chemicals

1. **Verify the SDS is on file before accepting delivery.**
   Check the chemical name against the SDS binder or digital library. If no SDS exists, contact the Safety Manager before signing for the shipment.

2. **Inspect containers for damage.**
   Look for dents, cracks, leaking seals, or illegible labels. Reject any damaged container — do not accept it into inventory.

3. **Log the chemical in the inventory system.**
   Record: chemical name, manufacturer, quantity, storage location, and date received.

### Labeling

4. **Never remove or deface original manufacturer labels.**
   If a label is damaged, apply a replacement label with the same information before storing.

5. **Label every secondary container.**
   Any chemical transferred from its original container into a spray bottle, bucket, or other vessel must be labeled with the chemical name and primary hazard (e.g., "Flammable," "Corrosive").

### Storage

6. **Segregate incompatible chemicals.**
   Store acids and bases on separate shelves with at least one empty shelf between them. Store flammables in the approved flammable cabinet only.

7. **Keep quantities to the minimum needed for one shift.**
   Return excess chemical to the storage room before the end of each shift.

8. **Do not store chemicals above eye level.**
   Maximum shelf height for any chemical container is 5 feet.

9. **Check storage areas weekly.**
   Look for leaks, expired chemicals (check "use by" or "date opened" labels), and containers that have migrated out of their designated zone.

### Spill Response

10. **Evacuate the immediate area.**
    Move all personnel at least 20 feet from the spill. Do not attempt to clean up an unknown substance.

11. **Identify the chemical using the SDS.**
    Locate the SDS for the spilled material. Confirm the correct cleanup method and PPE before approaching.

12. **Contain the spill with absorbent material.**
    Pour spill absorbent around the perimeter first, then work inward. Do not let the spill enter floor drains.

13. **Dispose of cleanup materials as chemical waste.**
    Bag absorbent material, label the bag with the chemical name, and place it in the designated hazardous waste container.

14. **Report every spill, even minor ones.**
    Complete an incident report within 2 hours of the spill.

### Disposal

15. **Never pour chemicals down the drain unless the SDS explicitly permits it.**
    Route all chemical waste to the hazardous waste staging area.

16. **Schedule waste pickup through the Safety Manager.**
    Do not accumulate hazardous waste for more than 90 days.

---

## Warnings

> **WARNING — Mixing bleach with ammonia-based cleaners produces toxic chloramine gas.** Symptoms include coughing, shortness of breath, and chest pain. If exposure occurs, move to fresh air immediately and call emergency services.

> **WARNING — Flammable chemicals must be stored in an approved metal cabinet away from ignition sources.** Do not store flammables near electrical panels, motors, or open flames.

---

## Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| SDS | Safety Data Sheet — a standardized document that describes a chemical's hazards, handling requirements, and emergency response. |
| Secondary container | Any container other than the original manufacturer packaging. |
| Incompatible chemicals | Chemicals that react dangerously if they contact each other (e.g., acid + base, oxidizer + flammable). |
| Hazardous waste staging area | The designated on-site location for chemical waste awaiting licensed disposal pickup. |
