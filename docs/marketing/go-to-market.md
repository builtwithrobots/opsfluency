## 1. Product Positioning

**Category:** Frontline knowledge and engagement platform for multilingual warehouse and manufacturing facilities.

**One-line pitch:**
> "Yourco broadcasts messages. OpsFluency enables competence."

**What it is not:** A translation tool. A learning management system. A compliance broadcaster. A per-seat HR platform.

**What it is:** Operations infrastructure. The system that gets the right procedure, in the right language, in front of the right worker, at the machine — without a supervisor standing there explaining it again.

### Positioning statement (internal, not for ads)

For operations managers at 50–500-worker bilingual facilities who are losing workers in the first 60 days and spending 17+ hours per week re-explaining procedures, OpsFluency is a frontline knowledge platform that publishes bilingual SOPs in under 15 minutes per document, delivers them via QR codes on the machines, and gives workers a no-download, no-password mobile app in their language — unlike generic LMS platforms or internal wikis that assume workers have laptops and speak English.

## 2. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Primary ICP: The Squeezed Middle Manager

**Title:** Operations Manager, Plant Manager, Production Supervisor, Warehouse Manager

**Company type:**
- Distribution centers, third-party logistics (3PL) providers
- Food and beverage manufacturing
- Consumer goods packaging and assembly
- Meat processing, cold-chain facilities
- Light manufacturing with repetitive station work

**Size:** 50–500 floor workers. Small enough that no IT department owns the process; large enough to feel the pain every week.

**Floor demographics:** 40–80% Spanish-speaking workforce. Significant number of workers for whom English-only onboarding is a friction point, not a failure of attention.

**Pain signals (what they say when they're ready to buy):**
- "I've rewritten this SOP three times and nobody reads it."
- "My Spanish-speaking workers aren't making mistakes because they don't care — they can't read the instructions."
- "We had an OSHA near-miss. I need proof workers are reading the safety procedures."
- "Turnover in the first 60 days is killing us and I don't know why."
- "My managers are spending half their shift babysitting things that should be self-service."

**Budget:** Under $200/month is expensable at the manager level in most facilities. No committee approval required.

**Buying signal:** They have existing SOPs already (binders, Word files, PDFs). They don't need to be convinced SOPs matter — they need them accessible.

### Secondary ICP: The HR / Safety Director

**Title:** EHS Manager, HR Director, Training Coordinator (at 200–500 worker facilities)

**Motivation:** OSHA compliance record, defensible training documentation, reduced incident rate.

**Entry point:** Safety checklist SOPs, lockout/tagout procedures, PPE documentation.

**Buy trigger:** Post-incident corrective action requirement, annual safety audit prep, or proactive OSHA compliance documentation. Key standards driving urgency: 29 CFR 1910.178 (forklifts — 3-year record retention, competency evaluation required), 1910.1200 (HAZCOM — written program must document how comprehension is verified), 1910.132 (PPE — employer must ascertain each employee understands proper use). OSHA compliance guidance is explicit: attendance sign-in sheets alone do not satisfy the comprehension verification requirement.

## 3. Key Messages

All copy follows the voice rule: **write like a plant manager talking to another plant manager.** Short sentences. Verbs over adjectives. Numbers when they exist.

### The problem message

Workers don't quit for fifty cents more per hour. They quit because they're frustrated and embarrassed. New hires nod through English-only training they don't understand, make avoidable mistakes, feel incompetent, and leave by week 8. Everyone blames pay. The real problem is dignity and competence.

Supervisors meanwhile spend 17+ hours a week re-explaining the same three procedures because there's no reliable self-service channel workers trust.

### The solution message

Upload your existing SOP. AI converts it to structured, readable Markdown in minutes. Your site-specific terms — conveyor names, equipment model numbers, internal acronyms — get flagged for you to define before a single word is translated. That glossary is yours, forever, and it's injected into every future translation. Approve the Spanish version. Publish. Print the QR code and stick it on the machine.

Worker scans the QR. Reads the procedure in their language. No app download. No password. Works offline.

Total manager time: under 15 minutes per SOP.

### The moat message (why not just use Google Translate)

Generic translation doesn't know that "the 3-line" means the third packaging conveyor at your facility, not Line 3 in a warehouse in another city. OpsFluency builds a company glossary the first time you define a term and injects it into every future translation. The same term, correctly translated, across every document. That consistency is the difference between a worker who trusts the procedure and one who has to verify it.

### The compliance message

"We had an OSHA near-miss. I need proof workers are reading the safety procedures." That's the sentence that turns an Operations Manager into a buyer and an EHS Director into a champion.

OSHA does not require a specific test score. It does require documented proof of comprehension — not just attendance. A sign-in sheet from a group training doesn't show that your forklift operator understood the lockout/tagout procedure. A timestamped quiz result with the exact questions asked, the answers given, and a pass/fail against a threshold you set — that does.

OpsFluency generates a 3-question bilingual comprehension check automatically when a procedure publishes. Workers take it on their phone after reading. The result is an immutable record: who, when, which version, which questions, what they answered, whether they passed. For most facilities, that exceeds what 29 CFR 1910.178, 1910.132, and 1910.1200 require. It's the difference between "we trained everyone" and "we can prove everyone understood."

*(Phase 2B feature — available at all pricing tiers. Advanced analytics and re-quiz workflows on Growth and Scale.)*

### Manifesto lines (approved for marketing surfaces)

- "Bilingual by default. Because your floor already is."
- "A QR code on the machine. The right SOP in the right language. Zero clicks to competence."
- "The translation runs once. The understanding compounds forever."

## 4. Competitive Differentiation

### The real competition

Most operations managers are not evaluating us against a named competitor. They are evaluating us against:

1. **Doing nothing** — SOPs in binders, tribal knowledge, supervisors re-explaining
2. **SharePoint / Google Drive** — searchable, but English-only, no QR, no mobile experience for workers without company email
3. **Generic LMS platforms** (Trainual, Lessonly, Confluence)** — built for desk workers, no bilingual publishing, no QR-to-machine workflow, per-seat pricing that scales badly for 200-person floors

### Why OpsFluency wins against each

| Comparison | Why we win |
|---|---|
| Binders / tribal knowledge | One QR code scan. Works offline. No supervisor required. Provable that the worker accessed the procedure. |
| SharePoint / Google Drive | No Spanish translation built in. No QR workflow. Workers don't have company email accounts. No offline mode. |
| Generic LMS (Trainual, Lessonly) | Per-seat pricing: 200 workers × $8/seat = $1,600/month vs. $119 flat. No bilingual publishing pipeline. Built for onboarding videos, not machine-mounted SOPs. |
| Dedicated translation services | Translation services produce PDFs. PDFs don't update when the procedure changes. No QR link, no worker app, no analytics, no glossary consistency across documents. |

### What we have that no one else combines

- **Company glossary** — site-specific terms defined once, injected into every future translation. This is not a feature; it is the compound interest engine of the platform.
- **QR-to-procedure, mounted at the machine** — the physical touchpoint that makes the digital content reachable on the floor without a laptop or company email.
- **Manager-approval gate on translations** — a manager signs off on the Spanish before it's live. This is not a liability hedge; it's the correct answer to "how do we know the Spanish is right."
- **Magic link auth with no passwords** — workers on the floor don't have password managers. Magic links expire, rotate, and can be resent in 30 seconds from the manager dashboard. No IT ticket required.
- **Comprehension records (Phase 2B)** — auto-generated bilingual quiz after every SOP publish. Immutable timestamped record of who passed, what questions they saw, in which language, against which SOP version. Covers 29 CFR 1910.178 (forklift), 1910.132 (PPE), and 1910.1200 (HAZCOM) documentation requirements. No competitor combines this with bilingual publishing and machine-mounted QR delivery.
- **Flat-rate pricing** — a 200-worker floor and a 350-worker floor pay the same $199/month. Adding workers doesn't break the budget.

## 5. Pricing

**Canonical pricing (PRD.md — use this):**

| Tier | Employees | Monthly (Annual) | Monthly (M-t-M) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Starter | Up to 50 | $79 | $99 |
| Growth | 51–150 | $119 | $149 |
| Scale | 151–500 | $199 | $249 |
| Enterprise | 500+ | Custom | Custom |

**Pricing principles:**

- **Flat-rate, not per-seat.** Every competitor prices per seat. Flat-rate is the first line of every sales conversation. "200 workers, $119/month, done."
- **Growth tier is the intended landing zone.** The vast majority of ICP customers are 50–150 worker facilities. $119/month is expensable without a VP signature at most manufacturing operators.
- **Annual discount is the conversion tool.** Month-to-month exists so buyers can start without a commitment. The annual pitch happens at 60 days when they've published their first 10 SOPs and can see the time savings.
- **Enterprise is a conversation, not a form.** 500+ worker facilities have IT requirements, SSO, custom SLAs. Enterprise pricing is quoted after a 30-minute call. Do not publish a number.

**What's included at every tier:**

- Unlimited SOPs
- Unlimited QR codes
- Bilingual publishing (English + Spanish)
- Worker PWA (unlimited workers within tier headcount)
- Monitor system (unlimited pairings)
- HR module
- Scan analytics
- Full AI conversion pipeline
- Comprehension quizzes — worker takes quiz + OSHA-compliant record + basic CSV export *(Phase 2B)*

## 6. Sales Motion

### Phase 1: Manual early customers (now → first 10 customers)

**Goal:** Prove the product works in real facilities. Get testimonials and case study material. Learn where the demo breaks.

**How to find them:**
- Personal networks: plant managers, ops directors, logistics coordinators
- LinkedIn: Operations Manager + (warehouse OR manufacturing OR distribution) + (bilingual OR Spanish OR Hispanic)
- Local manufacturing associations and industry groups (NAM, MHI chapters)
- OSHA training consultants who work with multilingual facilities

**Demo script:**

1. Start with the problem. Ask them about their first 60 days of turnover and how many hours their supervisors spend on re-explanation. Let them talk.
2. Show the import pipeline live. Upload a real SOP (offer to use one of theirs). Walk through the AI conversion, the glossary flagging, the Spanish approval.
3. Scan a QR code on your phone. Show the worker view. Toggle between English and Spanish.
4. Show the manager dashboard: published SOPs, scan analytics.
5. Price at the end: "For your size, it's $119/month flat. We'll start you on a 14-day trial."

**Early customer discount:** Offer 30% off first 6 months for customers who agree to a 30-minute case study call at 90 days. Cap at 5 customers. This is reference-building, not a discount program.

**Handshake SLA for early customers:** Response to any issue within 4 hours during business hours. You are the support. This is intentional — you need to hear what breaks.

### Phase 2: Self-serve (billing live → scalable)

**Trigger:** Billing (Stripe Checkout) implemented and tested. Free trial flow live. Marketing site pages built.

**Funnel:**

```
Organic / paid search / referral
    → Marketing site (problem page + features + pricing)
    → 14-day free trial (no card)
    → Onboarding: import first SOP + publish (aha moment)
    → Day 7 email: "You've published X SOPs. Your trial ends in 7 days."
    → Trial end: Growth tier prompt → card
    → Day 90: case study ask + annual upgrade prompt
```

**Conversion hypothesis:** The aha moment is publishing the first bilingual SOP and scanning the QR code with a phone. Everything in onboarding is designed to get to that moment in under 20 minutes on day one.

**Self-serve support:** In-app contextual help, email (support@opsfluency.com), and a public status page. No phone support at scale — phone support is reserved for Enterprise.

### Phase 3: Partner / channel (6–12 months out)

- OSHA training consultants — they work with exactly the ICP and have ongoing relationships. Revenue share model.
- 3PL operators — one sale covers all their client facilities if they standardize on OpsFluency.
- Workers' comp insurance brokers — bilingual safety documentation reduces claim frequency; they have incentive to recommend.

## 7. Channel Strategy

### Primary channels (launch)

**Direct outbound (Phase 1 manual):** Personal network + LinkedIn. High-touch, zero spend. Works until you have 10 customers.

**SEO — long-tail, problem-first:**

Target queries:
- "how to create bilingual SOPs for warehouse workers"
- "spanish sop for manufacturing"
- "employee training spanish english warehouse"
- "OSHA training bilingual employees"
- "reduce turnover new hire manufacturing"

Content angle: Write for the problem, not the product. "Why your warehouse workers quit in the first 60 days" outperforms "bilingual SOP software" at this stage.

**LinkedIn organic:** The ICP is on LinkedIn. Short posts from the founder perspective: problem-framing posts, behind-the-scenes of how the AI pipeline works, real customer stories (anonymized until they agree to a case study). No growth-hacking tactics — write like a plant manager, for plant managers.

### Secondary channels (post-billing)

**Google Ads:** Only after self-serve is live and the free trial converts. Avoid ads on a broken funnel.

**Product Hunt:** Launch for visibility in the SaaS community, not the ICP. Useful for early feedback and backlinks.

**Industry associations:** NAM (National Association of Manufacturers), MHI (Material Handling Institute), OSHA Alliance partners. Sponsoring a webinar for a state manufacturing association ($500–$1,500) reaches exactly the right audience.

**Referral program:** At scale, offer one free month per referred customer who completes a trial. Simple, no affiliate software needed — handle manually until volume justifies automation.

## 8. Launch Readiness Checklist

These are the blockers between "working product" and "sellable product." Everything else can ship after first revenue.

### Must-have before taking money

- [ ] **Billing** — Stripe Checkout integrated. Free trial → paid conversion flow. Subscription management (upgrade, cancel). Webhook handling for failed payments.
- [ ] **Terms of Service** — Simple, plain-English ToS covering data ownership, acceptable use, limitation of liability. ($500 lawyer review or use a vetted template service.)
- [ ] **Privacy Policy** — CCPA-compliant (California), GDPR-ready (if any EU customers). Employee PII section covering what biometric/location data is NOT collected (there is none, but say it).
- [ ] **Security page** (`/security` or `/trust`) — Use the approved trust block from `docs/branding/README.md §9`. Include the "what we can truthfully claim" checklist.
- [ ] **Support email** — support@opsfluency.com, monitored. Can be a shared inbox at launch.

### Must-have to run a demo

- [ ] **Demo environment** — A seeded demo account with 3–5 published SOPs, scan history, and a company glossary. Use realistic warehouse content (forklift safety, PPE checklist, receiving dock procedure). Not "Lorem Ipsum SOP."
- [ ] **QR print** — Tested on a real printer at Letter size. QR must scan reliably when printed at 2" and mounted.
- [ ] **Mobile PWA** — Tested on iOS Safari and Android Chrome. The QR scan → worker view → language toggle flow must work without friction.

### Should-have before marketing site launch

- [ ] **Marketing site homepage** — Problem + solution + pricing + trust strip + CTA. No feature tour yet; get to the demo CTA fast.
- [ ] **Pricing page** — Tier table with flat-rate emphasis, annual vs. month-to-month toggle, free trial CTA. Enterprise → "Talk to us" link.
- [ ] **Case study / testimonial** — Even one. A quote and a logo is enough. Get this from the first manual customer.
- [ ] **Onboarding email sequence** — Day 0 (welcome), Day 7 (trial check-in), Day 13 (trial expiring), Day 14 (conversion prompt). Plain text is fine.

### Nice-to-have at 30 days post-launch

- [ ] **Help center** — 10–15 articles covering the most common questions from Phase 1 customers. Hosted on Intercom or Notion (public) — don't build custom.
- [ ] **Status page** — status.opsfluency.com via Betterstack or Upptime. One hour to set up. Customers ask.
- [ ] **Comprehension quizzes** (Phase 2B) — Auto-generated bilingual quiz on every published SOP. Produces immutable timestamped records that satisfy 29 CFR 1910.178 (forklift), 1910.132 (PPE), and 1910.1200 (HAZCOM) documentation requirements. OSHA requires proof of comprehension, not just attendance — this is the proof. Primary sales accelerator for EHS/Safety Director buyers. Available at all tiers; management analytics on Growth+.
- [ ] **Translation Memory stats** in manager dashboard — lets managers see the cost savings compounding. Reinforces the glossary value story.

## 10. What We Do Not Say

These phrases are banned from all OpsFluency marketing copy:

| Banned phrase | Why |
|---|---|
| "Transform your workforce" | Generic B2B SaaS filler. We're not that. |
| "Empower your employees" | Says nothing. Everyone says this. |
| "AI-driven insights" | Vague and trend-chasing. We use AI to do a specific thing: convert SOPs. Say that instead. |
| "Enterprise-grade security" | Meaningless. Name the mechanism: Row Level Security, HTTPS, magic link. |
| "Content" (for SOPs) | SOPs are procedures, not content. "Content" is for marketing teams. |
| "Journeys" / "experiences" / "wellness" | L&D buzzwords. Our buyers are plant managers. |
| "Platform" as a standalone noun | "The platform" means nothing. Say what it does. |
| "(ES)" label next to Spanish | Spanish is not a translation. It stands alone. |
