## Founder Voice Rules

### The core rule
**Rob's voice is first-person and floor-native.** All OpsFluency copy should feel like it came from someone who has been on a warehouse floor at 2am trying to figure out why the pick rate dropped. Not from a marketing department. Not from a generic "thought leader."

### What Rob sounds like

| He says | He does not say |
|---|---|
| "I've rewritten that SOP three times and nobody read it — I get it." | "We understand the challenges facing today's workforce." |
| "The problem isn't pay. It's dignity and competence." | "Employee empowerment drives engagement." |
| "A 200-worker floor at $119/month. Done." | "Scalable, cost-effective solutions for enterprise operations." |
| "I ran three sites at once. I know what breaks." | "Leveraging synergies across multi-location deployments." |
| "I built this because the tool didn't exist when I needed it." | "Disrupting the operations intelligence category." |
| "Your floor already speaks two languages. Your systems should too." | "Enabling bilingual workforce solutions." |

### Voice attributes — with examples

**Direct, not diplomatic.**
- Does: "Most LMS platforms are built for desk workers. Yours aren't desk workers."
- Does not: "Traditional learning management solutions may not fully address the needs of all workforce types."

**Credentialed, not credentialing.**
He doesn't list his resume in every sentence. He lets the results do the talking and drops specific numbers when they're relevant.
- Does: "We hit >99% accuracy at the Sparks facility. It took six months and a lot of SOP rewrites. I know what works."
- Does not: "As a Director of Operations with 22 years of experience, I bring a wealth of knowledge to every engagement."

**Peer-to-peer, not vendor-to-buyer.**
He writes to plant managers as a peer who has the same problems, not as a salesperson who has a product to pitch.
- Does: "You're not losing workers to the competitor across town. You're losing them to week 8 confusion. I've fixed that."
- Does not: "Our platform addresses the key pain points of modern warehouse management."

**Specific, not vague.**
Numbers, procedures, equipment, real scenarios.
- Does: "Three questions. Timed. Bilingual. Result is immutable. That's your OSHA proof-of-comprehension record."
- Does not: "Comprehensive compliance documentation for regulatory requirements."

## Photo Direction — Rob's Portrait

When a portrait of Rob is used on the website, sales materials, or LinkedIn:

- **Setting:** Facility preferred over office. Warehouse floor, dock area, or manufacturing line — a setting that signals he's been there.
- **Clothing:** Working attire appropriate to the setting. Not a suit. Not a startup hoodie. A fleece or vest over a collared shirt is correct.
- **Expression:** Direct eye contact. Not smiling for the camera. The expression of someone who has solved a hard problem before and will again.
- **Avoid:** Studio white backgrounds, hard-hat worn backwards for the photographer, posed laptop-holding, "thought leader arms crossed" pose.
- **Format:** Landscape crop preferred for homepage hero. Square crop for LinkedIn and email signature.
