---

**Copywriter notes:**
- "Your floor is running on tribal knowledge." — every ops manager reads this and recognizes themselves. It does not accuse; it diagnoses. It is the correct opening line.
- The subheadline leads with time (22 years) then proves it (warehouses, plants, 3PLs) then pivots to the offer (OpsFluency). Do not reorder.
- Primary CTA is always "Talk to Rob" — never "Contact Us," "Get Started," or "Learn More."
- The trust strip is not a bulleted list — it is a one-line inline credential row. Three items maximum.

---

### Section 2: The Problem

**Section label (small caps, teal):**
> What I hear from every new client

**Problem block — three pain points, each with a quote + translation:**

**Pain point 1:**
> "My Spanish-speaking workers keep making the same mistakes. I don't think they're reading the instructions."
>
> *They're not. The instructions are in English.*

**Pain point 2:**
> "I've rewritten this SOP three times. Nobody uses it."
>
> *That's a delivery problem, not a content problem. The SOP needs to reach the worker at the machine, in their language, without a supervisor standing there.*

**Pain point 3:**
> "We had an OSHA near-miss. I need proof workers are reading the safety procedures — not just attending the training."
>
> *A sign-in sheet is not proof of comprehension. I can show you what is.*

**Pain point 4 (optional, can cut for space):**
> "Turnover in the first 60 days is killing us and I don't know why."
>
> *Workers don't quit for fifty cents more per hour. They quit because they feel incompetent. That's fixable.*

---

**Copywriter notes:**
- The quote format ("I hear this from every new client") positions Rob as a peer who listens, not a vendor who pitches.
- Each pain point uses the customer's exact words followed by Rob's direct response. Short. No hedging.
- The responses do not pitch a product. They diagnose the root cause. The product comes later.
- Do not add more than four pain points. Four is already a lot to read. Three is ideal.
